## ğŸ“ Understanding What You're Doing

### What is a Transaction?

When you click "Submit Score" or "Mint NFT", you're:
1. **Creating a transaction** on the blockchain
2. **Paying a small fee** (gas) to the network
3. **Permanently recording** your action

**This is YOU operating YOUR bank.** The gas fee? That's like paying the electricity to run your bank's computers!

### What is Gas?

**Gas = Network Fee**

- It's like a transaction fee, but you're paying the blockchain network
- Amount varies based on network congestion
- On testnets (like we're using), it's free tBTC
- On mainnet, it's real money (but still usually small)

**Think of it as:** Paying for the fuel (gas) that powers the blockchain computers.

### What is a Smart Contract?

The QuizContract and NFT contract are **programs living on the blockchain.**

- They automatically execute when conditions are met
- NO ONE can change them (even the creator!)
- They're transparent (anyone can see the code)
- They're trustless (no need to trust a person)

## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips from the Web3 Veterans

### Tip 1: Hardware Wallet for Serious Amounts

Once you're ready to use mainnet with real money:
- Consider a **hardware wallet** (Ledger, Trezor)
- Keeps private keys on physical device
- Much safer than software wallet
- Think: Your savings go in hardware, spending money in MetaMask

### Tip 2: Multiple Wallets for Different Purposes

**Hot wallet** (MetaMask on phone): Daily spending, small amounts  
**Warm wallet** (MetaMask on computer): Medium amounts, apps you trust  
**Cold wallet** (Hardware wallet): Life savings, long-term holdings  

Like real life: You don't carry your life savings in your physical wallet, right?

### Tip 3: Revoke Contract Approvals

When you interact with contracts, you often "approve" them to access your tokens.

**Problem:** Old approvals can be security risks!

**Solution:** Regularly check and revoke old approvals at:
- https://revoke.cash
- https://approved.zone

Takes 5 minutes, massively improves security!
